I cant remember what was our first computer, but my first memories of personal computer is a huge crt on the table and im there playing some thomas the tank engine puzzle game. I was 3 years old at that point.

When I was around 6, I learned that my uncle is a gamedev. Back then he worked on a game series called Trials. A motorcycle game. (Nowdays he finnished working on Alan Wake 2 few years ago. and to be honest I dont know what he is working on at the moment.)

You can imagine what that seemed like to a kid, he was cooler then anyone. He was also our familys techsupport. (Oh how do I feel hes pain nowdays).

I feel bad for my mom, who had to call him every half a year when I had bricked our family computer trying to download a games for free from virus sited and god knows where.

So as you might imagine I was not great with computers, but I was addicted to computers.

I had to fight with my big sister on who can be on a computer...I was 7 and she was 16. You can imagine her IRCgallery and Windows messenger time was more important then my ass watching smosh, pewdiepie

Years went by, I always fought with my little brother on who gets to break moms computer trying to download shit we dont understand.

When I was 13 I found counter strike and that was something else. I went on this churchcamp, after that family members give you money and I spend all of that on a new computer.

It was such a shit computer intel i5 i think it was 8 or 9 gen, nvidia 960 if I remember correctly, 240gb ssd (ssd is the only thing that is still inside my current build)

I had razer deathstalker keyboard, wich was probably the worst gamning keyboard you could get, deathadder 2013 mouse wich was great and then steelseries siberia v2 headset wich was amazing(atleast now remembering it through nostalgia)

My monitor was an old tv and I played cs with 30fps, lowest resolutions, 60ping, but I played my hearth out.

Ever since I started there was nothing like it. I went straight to my pc when I got home from school, I played cs until my mom yelled me to take my ass to bed.

(fun fact right now I have around 8000hours in CS, and I still love the game.) ((I do hate cs2 tho. RIP GO))

Back to the times I started CS, I was in secondary school, there was an computer course. It was HTML coding.

I fucking sucked at it. I copy pasted everything from my friend who somehow knew how to google shit better then I did.

I like how im writing about that course, while doing a html site trying to remember those commands from that course.

Now you know I was addicted to computers since I was 7 and now we added addiction of counter strike to the mix.

Back then I had great grades,I was planning on going to high school. But then at 14 years old we had a visiting day in a vocational college near my home.

First thing I see is a classroom of computers, all the students playing cs 1.6 together. I was hooked. I asked what kind of grades you need to get it.

The relief I felt when I heard I could drop my almost 9 grades to an humble 7. That ment the last year of school, I started kicking back, no more sweating. I knew what I wanted and it was to work with computers.

The studies themself dont really matter on my story, but the work I did when I was training for school. I worked in technical support, first few weeks at phone service, untill they realised I dont know how to do anything.

Then I got kicked to onsite support, oh how I loved it. I got my own desk, I started going through tickets. I changed hdmi cables, installed operatingsystems for the first time, touched computer parts for the first time.

I learned so much while working there, but I also learned alot about pending the rules from few of my coworkers. I still havent listed the client I worked for and im pretty sure I signed a NDA, but I was 16 who was still partying and playing cs when I wasent drunk or working. I dont remember anything I have signed.

Anyways after I spend almost a year working there, I got into a slump in IT. The school didint really teach me those things I wanted to learn, but I was so close to graduate I had to see it through.

I think around this time I bought new parts for my PC. I got 10th gen i5, 1080ti, 144hz monitor...I was living and I was gaming.

It got so bad she thought I was doing cocain, due to me playing all the nights. She just didint know I was not at school when she was working. I was resting and gathering energy to play some more cs.
